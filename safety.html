At the Airport
Stay alert and prepared throughout your airport experience
1

Arrive Early

Arrive at least 3 hours early for international flights and 2 hours for domestic. Allow extra time during peak seasons.

2

Keep Documents Handy

Keep your passport, visa, and boarding pass accessible at all times. Use a travel wallet to avoid fumbling at security.

3

Watch Your Belongings

Never leave bags unattended. Report suspicious packages to airport security immediately. Use luggage locks.

4

Use Official Taxis

Only use pre-paid taxis or airport-authorized ride-sharing services. Avoid unauthorized drivers outside the terminal.

5

Secure Your Devices

Don't use public USB charging stations — use your own power bank. Be cautious on public airport Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.

6

Know Your Gate

Confirm your gate immediately after check-in and after clearing security. Gates can change — check the display boards regularly.

At Your Destination
Stay safe and aware wherever your travels take you
1

Research Local Laws

Understand local rules around photography, dress codes, religious sites, and customs. What's normal at home may be offensive or illegal abroad.

2

Keep Copies of Documents

Carry photocopies of your passport, visa, and insurance. Store digital copies in a secure cloud service accessible from any device.

3

Register with Your Embassy

For extended stays abroad, register with your country's embassy or consulate. They can assist in emergencies, lost passports, and evacuations.

4

Avoid Sharing Itinerary Publicly

Don't post real-time location updates on social media. Wait until you've left a location to share photos and updates.

5

Use ATMs Safely

Use ATMs in well-lit, busy areas or inside bank branches. Cover the keypad when entering your PIN and check for skimming devices.

6

Trust Your Instincts

If a situation feels unsafe, leave immediately. Avoid dark alleys at night, keep hotel address written in local language, and stay aware of your surroundings.

7

Protect Against Scams

Be wary of "free" tours, unsolicited help with luggage, or people asking to take photos with you. Research common scams for your destination beforehand.

8

Stay Connected

Get a local SIM or international roaming plan. Share your daily plans with someone back home. Keep your phone charged at all times.

Health & Wellness
Keep yourself healthy and energized throughout your trip
1

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water, especially on long flights. Airplane cabins have very low humidity — aim for 8+ glasses per day while flying.

2

Carry a First Aid Kit

Pack basic medications: paracetamol, antacids, antihistamines, bandages, and any prescription medicines. Carry a doctor's letter for prescription drugs.

3

Food Safety

Be cautious with street food in high-risk destinations. Stick to cooked food, bottled water, and avoid raw salads in areas with poor sanitation.

4

Manage Jet Lag

Adjust sleep schedule 2–3 days before travel. Stay awake until local bedtime at destination. Avoid alcohol and caffeine on the flight.

5

Sun Protection

Use SPF 30+ sunscreen in tropical destinations. Wear hats and UV-protective clothing. Seek shade during peak sun hours (10 AM–4 PM).

6

Insect Protection

Use DEET-based insect repellent in malaria or dengue-prone regions. Sleep under mosquito nets if provided. Check travel advisories for disease alerts.

General Health Guidelines for Travellers
Key health information to keep in mind before and during your trip
What vaccinations do I need for international travel?
▼
Required vaccinations vary by destination. Common recommended vaccines include:
  • Yellow Fever: Required for entry to many African and South American countries
  • Hepatitis A & B: Recommended for most international travel
  • Typhoid: Recommended for South Asia and some parts of Africa
  • Malaria prophylaxis: Required medication for sub-Saharan Africa and parts of Asia
  • COVID-19: Check current entry requirements for your destination
Consult a travel medicine doctor at least 4–6 weeks before departure.
How do I handle a medical emergency abroad?
▼
In a medical emergency: (1) Call the local emergency number (see table above); (2) Contact your travel insurance provider — they usually have 24/7 helplines; (3) Notify your hotel or accommodation for assistance; (4) Contact your country's nearest embassy or consulate. Always keep your insurance policy number and emergency contact handy. Most travel insurance plans cover medical evacuation if needed.
How can I avoid traveller's diarrhea?
▼
Traveller's diarrhea is the most common travel illness. To prevent it:
  • Drink only bottled or purified water — even for brushing teeth in high-risk areas
  • Avoid ice cubes unless made from purified water
  • Eat only thoroughly cooked food from reputable establishments
  • Wash hands frequently with soap or use alcohol-based sanitizer
  • Carry oral rehydration salts and basic antibiotics as advised by your doctor
What should I know about flying with medical conditions?
▼
Passengers with medical conditions should consult their doctor before flying. Key points:
  • Cabin pressure at altitude can affect blood oxygen levels — relevant for heart/lung conditions
  • DVT (deep vein thrombosis) risk increases on long flights — wear compression socks, walk regularly
  • Carry all medications in hand luggage with prescription documentation
  • Inform the airline of conditions requiring wheelchair assistance, oxygen, or special meals
  • Carry a medical summary letter from your doctor in English
How do I stay safe during extreme weather events while travelling?
▼
Monitor weather forecasts daily for your destination. Register with your country's embassy for alerts. If a severe weather event occurs: stay indoors and follow local authorities' instructions; contact your airline for flight changes (most waive fees during declared emergencies); contact TravelWise support for hotel cancellation assistance during natural disasters. Travel insurance typically covers trip interruption due to natural disasters.
