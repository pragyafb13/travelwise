Know Before You Go
Destination Safety Tips
Region-specific advice curated by our travel safety experts and updated regularly to reflect current conditions.
🇮🇳

India

Monsoon travel, local SIM cards, trusted transport apps
▼

India is a vast and diverse country offering extraordinary travel experiences. Being prepared for regional differences — especially during monsoon season (June–September) — ensures a smooth and safe trip.

Avoid low-lying coastal routes and mountain roads during peak monsoon (July–Aug) — landslides are common in the Western Ghats and Himalayan regions.
Pick up a local SIM card at the airport — Airtel and Jio offer affordable tourist-friendly prepaid plans with strong 4G coverage across major cities.
Use only app-based cabs (Ola, Uber, Rapido) or pre-paid taxis from official airport booths — avoid unmarked cabs.
Always drink bottled or purified water — even at reputable hotels. Avoid ice in beverages at roadside stalls.
Carry a printed hotel address in the local language when exploring rural or off-the-beaten-path areas.
Register at the FRRO portal (foreigners.gov.in) if staying more than 14 days as a foreign national.
🌏

Southeast Asia

Scam awareness, food safety, drinking water
▼

Southeast Asia — Thailand, Bali, Vietnam, Cambodia and beyond — is breathtaking but comes with tourist-targeted scams. Stay alert and informed before you travel.

Beware the "gem scam" and "tuk-tuk redirect" — if a stranger offers an unsolicited "special deal", it's almost always a scam.
Book tours through licensed operators or your hotel concierge — never pay upfront to street touts.
Eat street food cooked fresh in front of you. Avoid raw salads and unpeeled fruits from roadside vendors.
Drink only sealed bottled water — even brushing teeth with tap water can cause stomach problems in some regions.
Keep your bag in front of you on motorbikes — drive-by bag snatching is common in major tourist cities.
Get Hepatitis A and Typhoid vaccinations at least 6 weeks before departure — consult a travel health doctor.
🇪🇺

Europe

Pickpocket hotspots, emergency numbers (112), travel insurance
▼

Europe is generally very safe but pickpocketing remains a significant issue in major tourist cities like Rome, Barcelona, Paris, and Prague. Stay smart, stay aware.

High-risk zones: Barcelona's La Rambla, Rome's Colosseum area, Paris Metro Line 1, Prague Old Town Square — always wear bags in front.
Use a money belt or RFID-blocking wallet; keep passports and extra cards locked in the hotel safe.
Pan-European emergency number 112 works from any phone (even without a SIM card) in all EU countries.
Travel insurance is strongly recommended — European medical care is excellent but extremely expensive for uninsured visitors.
Register with your country's embassy when traveling in regions with political instability or active advisories.
Beware distraction techniques — a "dropped item" or sudden crowd surge near attractions is often used to conceal theft.
🕌

Middle East

Dress code awareness, photography restrictions, local laws
▼

Countries like the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, and Qatar are increasingly welcoming to international visitors, but require cultural awareness and deep respect for local customs and laws.

Dress modestly in public — cover shoulders and knees. Women should carry a light scarf for mosque and religious site visits.
Always ask permission before photographing people, government buildings, military installations, or places of worship.
Public displays of affection are illegal in many Middle Eastern countries — even between married couples.
Alcohol laws vary widely — strictly prohibited in Saudi Arabia, but available in licensed venues in UAE and Jordan.
During Ramadan, eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours is prohibited in some countries for all visitors.
Some medications (certain painkillers, anti-anxiety drugs) require a doctor's letter translated into Arabic — check before packing.

Stay Healthy Abroad
COVID & Health Advisories
Current travel health information, vaccination recommendations, and entry requirements — reviewed and updated monthly.
💉

Vaccination Requirements

Most destinations no longer require COVID-19 vaccination proof for entry, but Yellow Fever and Hepatitis A vaccines are still recommended for travel to parts of Africa, South America, and South/Southeast Asia.

Up to Date — Mar 2026
🦠

Current Health Alerts

WHO has issued advisories for Mpox in parts of Central Africa. Dengue Fever remains active across South/Southeast Asia and Latin America. Consult your travel doctor 6–8 weeks before departure.

Monitor Closely
📋

Entry Requirements

Entry requirements have largely normalized post-COVID. Some countries still require proof of travel health insurance. Always verify current requirements on the official embassy or consulate website of your destination.

Verify Before Travel
🧳

Travel Health Kit

Pack ORS sachets, prescription broad-spectrum antibiotics, antidiarrheal medication, a thermometer, hand sanitizer, and all personal prescriptions with an accompanying doctor's note in English.
