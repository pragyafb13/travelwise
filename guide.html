Traveller Essentials

Quick Tips Every Traveller Needs

Smart advice to travel smarter, safer, and stress-free every time.

🛡️

Travel Insurance

Always get comprehensive travel insurance covering medical emergencies, trip cancellation, and baggage loss. Compare plans on platforms like PolicyBazaar for India-specific needs. Budget ₹500–₹2,000 depending on duration and destination.

🛂

Visa Information

Check visa requirements 3–6 months ahead for international trips. Indian passport holders get visa-on-arrival in 60+ countries. Use official embassy websites or trusted services like iVisa for accurate, up-to-date requirements.

💱

Currency & Money

Use Forex cards or multi-currency wallets for international travel — avoid airport exchange counters which charge 5–8% markup. Notify your bank before travel. Keep a small amount of local cash for emergencies and tipping.

💊

Health & Vaccinations

Visit a travel clinic 4–8 weeks before departure. Common vaccines for SE Asia include Hep A, Typhoid, and Japanese Encephalitis. Carry a basic med kit: ORS, antacids, antihistamines, pain relief, and your prescriptions.

📱

Connectivity Tips

Buy a local SIM on arrival for best rates, or use an eSIM service like Airalo for instant connectivity. Download Google Maps offline for your destination. Use WhatsApp or Signal for free international messaging.

🏨

Accommodation Hacks

Book refundable rates and cancel if you find better deals. Hostels offer great social experiences for solo travellers. For longer stays, Airbnb or serviced apartments beat hotels on value. Always read reviews from the last 3 months.

✈️

Flight Strategy

Tuesday–Wednesday departures are typically cheapest. Book 6–8 weeks ahead for domestic, 3–4 months for international. Set price alerts on Google Flights. Use the 24-hr rule: airlines must allow free cancellation within 24 hrs of booking.

🌿

Sustainable Travel

Choose trains over flights for short distances, carry a reusable bottle and bag, stay in locally-owned guesthouses, eat at local restaurants, and avoid single-use plastics. Offset your carbon footprint through certified providers.

Common Questions

Travel FAQs

Quick answers to the questions every traveller asks.

How far in advance should I book international flights? +
For international flights, booking 2–4 months in advance typically yields the best prices. Popular routes during peak season (Dec–Jan, summer) may require booking 5–6 months ahead. Use Google Flights price tracking to monitor fare changes and book when you see a significant dip.
What travel insurance do I really need? +
At minimum, get medical coverage (minimum USD 1,00,000 for international), trip cancellation, and baggage insurance. For adventure activities like trekking or scuba diving, ensure your policy covers them. Single-trip policies work for occasional travel; annual multi-trip plans save money if you travel 3+ times per year.
How do I avoid forex fees and currency scams? +
Use a multi-currency Forex card from HDFC, ICICI, or Thomas Cook for locked-in rates. Avoid exchanging at airports — rates are 5–8% worse. In popular tourist areas, politely refuse 'dynamic currency conversion' at ATMs, which charges extra fees. Always pay in local currency when using cards abroad.
Is it safe to travel solo in India and abroad? +
Solo travel can be incredibly rewarding with the right precautions. Share your itinerary with family/friends, stay in well-reviewed accommodations, trust your instincts, and keep copies of all documents. Join travel communities on Reddit and Facebook for destination-specific safety advice. For women solo travellers, apps like bSafe and community groups provide extra support.
What is the best way to experience local food safely? +
Eat where locals eat — busy, bustling spots with high turnover usually mean fresh food. Avoid pre-cut fruits and raw salads in high-risk regions. Street food cooked fresh in front of you is often safer than buffets. Always drink sealed bottled water or use a LifeStraw filter bottle. Start with milder dishes if your stomach isn't acclimatised.
How do I get a visa for popular international destinations? +
Visa processes vary widely. For Schengen (Europe), apply through the embassy of your primary destination country. Thailand, Malaysia, and many SE Asian countries offer visa-on-arrival or e-visa. UK, USA, Canada require advance applications (6–12 weeks). Always check official embassy websites or use services like iVisa or VFS Global for guided assistance.
What should I do if my passport or wallet is lost/stolen? +
First, file a police report — you'll need this for insurance claims. Contact the nearest Indian Embassy/Consulate immediately for emergency travel documents. Block your cards via mobile banking apps. Share your digital document copies (stored in Google Drive or email) with someone at home. Most travel insurance covers emergency document replacement expenses.
