Essential Tips

Smart Travel Essentials

Key things every traveller should know before packing their bags.

🛡️

Travel Insurance

Always buy before you fly. A comprehensive policy covers medical emergencies, cancellations, and lost baggage — peace of mind starts here.

📋

Visa Requirements

Check 90 days in advance. Visa processing times vary widely by country. Apply early and double-check embassy requirements.

💱

Currency Tips

Use multi-currency cards to avoid foreign transaction fees. Keep a small amount of local cash for markets, taxis, and tips.

💉

Health & Vaccines

Consult your doctor 6 weeks prior. Some destinations require proof of vaccination — Yellow Fever and Hepatitis are common requirements.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions every traveller asks.

For domestic flights, booking 4–8 weeks in advance usually gets you the best prices. For international flights, aim for 2–6 months ahead. Prices spike in the final 2 weeks before departure, so the earlier the better for long-haul routes.

While not always legally mandatory, travel insurance is highly recommended. Some countries (like Schengen-area Europe) require proof of minimum medical coverage. Even where optional, it protects against trip cancellations, medical emergencies, and lost luggage — often costing less than a single meal out.

At minimum: a valid passport (ideally 6+ months validity), your visa or e-visa confirmation, travel insurance certificate, return flight tickets, and hotel booking confirmation. Some countries also require vaccination certificates — always check the destination country's official embassy website.

Keep enough local currency for your first day (transport, food, tips) — roughly $50–100 equivalent. Use ATMs at your destination for the rest, as they usually offer better exchange rates than airports. Always keep backup cash in a separate location.

In cities and tourist areas, major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard) are widely accepted. However, rural areas, local markets, and street food stalls often operate cash-only. Southeast Asia and parts of India especially prefer cash for small transactions. Always carry some local currency as backup.

Avoid airport kiosks — they offer the worst rates with high fees. Best options are: multi-currency cards like Niyo or Forex cards with zero markup, local ATMs at your destination, or authorised money changers in the city. Always decline "dynamic currency conversion" when paying by card abroad.

Share your itinerary with someone at home, keep digital copies of all documents in cloud storage, use hotel safes for passports and extra cash, research common local scams before you arrive, and buy comprehensive travel insurance. Register with your country's embassy website for advisories in high-risk destinations.
